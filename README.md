
# Trader Performance vs Market Sentiment Analysis

## Project Objective
This project analyzes trader performance and behavior under different market sentiment conditions (Fear vs Greed). The goal is to understand how market sentiment affects profitability, trading behavior, and trader performance.

## Methodology
1. Cleaned both datasets.
2. Converted date columns to proper datetime format.
3. Merged historical trading data with Fear & Greed index data using date.
4. Created sentiment categories (Fear vs Greed).
5. Calculated performance metrics such as:
   - Total PnL
   - Average PnL
   - Trade Frequency
   - Position Size
   - Long vs Short trades
6. Compared trader performance and behavior across Fear and Greed market conditions.
7. Created charts and tables for analysis.

## Insights
1. Trader profitability is higher during Greed market conditions.
2. Traders trade more frequently during Fear markets.
3. Position sizes are generally larger during Greed periods.
4. Short positions are more common during Fear markets.
5. Market sentiment clearly influences trader behavior and performance.

## Strategy Recommendations
1. During Fear markets, traders should reduce position sizes and avoid overtrading due to higher volatility and lower profitability.
2. During Greed markets, traders should increase position sizes and use trend-following strategies to capture market momentum.
3. Traders should adjust trading frequency and position size based on market sentiment.

## How to Run
1. Open the Jupyter Notebook.
2. Run all cells step by step.
3. The analysis, charts, and insights will be generated automatically.

## Conclusion
Market sentiment (Fear vs Greed) has a significant impact on trader performance, trading frequency, position sizes, and long/short behavior. Traders can improve performance by adjusting their strategies based on market sentiment.
